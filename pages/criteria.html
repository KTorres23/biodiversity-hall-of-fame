BHOF Induction Criteria

How we recognize, encourage, and award outstanding young naturalists in the educational system.

The 100 Unique Observations Rule

To incentivize generalist observation strategies and discourage students from simply capturing the same species repeatedly, candidates must meet specific taxonomic quotas.

Induction requires at least 100 unique Research-Grade Observations on iNaturalist. Out of those observations, a minimum of 5 observations must be verified in each of the following groups:

🌿 Plants
🐞 Invertebrates
🐸 Amphibians
🐢 Reptiles
🐟 Fish
🐦 Birds
🦊 Mammals

🏆 Leaderboard Jurisdictions

Students compete and earn places in Halls of Fame modeled after sports institutions:

  • Top 3: Local school or jurisdiction induction.
  • Top 10: State-wide recognition.
  • Top 50: Ecoregional induction.

*Compilations can be tracked for both students and adult mentors.
