Getting Started

Select your track and role below to unlock your custom onboarding path and discover relevant project tools.

🎒 High School Student Pathway

Learn about nature in your area, join a student conservation community, and work toward local or state BHOF induction.

  • Download iNaturalist and create an account. Ask your teacher or parent if you need help with setup.
  • Join your school's **Biodiversity Club** or active class project workspace.
  • Explore nearby parks or school grounds to submit observations. Remember to only document **wild** species.
  • Strive for **100 unique Research-Grade observations** across taxonomic groups (Plants, Birds, Bugs, etc.) to qualify for induction.
  • Find other active high schools on the map below to see what species they are finding!

🏫 High School Educator Pathway

Bring field biology to life in your classroom. Set up healthy competitions to motivate student research.

  • Download the **School Integration Kit** for templates on lesson plans and syllabi.
  • Set up an iNaturalist **Project** for your specific high school district.
  • Start a student-led **Biodiversity Club** to host local practice bioblitzes.
  • Encourage students to collaborate on identifications to raise observations to "Research-Grade".
  • Coordinate with neighboring schools to establish inter-school leaderboard cups.
Download Integration Kit Contact for Support

🎓 University Student Pathway

Engage in high-value citizen science, support regional ecoregion mapping, and compete for collegiate BHOF recognition.

  • Create an iNaturalist account and link it to your college or university's BHOF network page.
  • Learn to annotate observations (life stage, phenology, sex) to add value for professional researchers.
  • Help identify other users' uploads to help build community consensus (Research-Grade status).
  • Check the network map below to connect with other active campus groups and share local collections data.

🏛️ University Faculty & Advisor Pathway

Incorporate citizen science datasets into your ecology or conservation courses. Form campus biodiversity clubs.

  • Integrate BHOF criteria directly into field biology courses or lab requirements.
  • Establish an iNaturalist **Umbrella Project** to track all observations within your university properties.
  • Co-advise the campus **Biodiversity Club** and help connect students to local land managers.
  • Connect with other universities in the BHOF network map below to establish collaborative research collections or cooperative projects.

🔍 For Researchers

Leverage student-generated, research-grade datasets to support biodiversity studies, evolutionary ecology, and regional baseline audits.

  • Query high-quality species occurrences mapped by local ecoregions.
  • Identify baseline data points for species of Greatest Conservation Need.
  • Use students as active field collectors for targeted bioblitz datasets.

  • Establish Baseline projects for ecoregion-level tracking.
